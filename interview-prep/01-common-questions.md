# Interview Prep — Common Questions

Practice these questions out loud. Don't just read them — say your answer.

A good answer is: short, clear, and honest.

---

## Tell me about yourself

**What they want to hear:** Who you are, your background, why you are here.

**Model answer:**
> "My name is [Your Name]. I am a beginner frontend developer who has been learning HTML, CSS, JavaScript, TypeScript, and Angular. I built a small Task Manager project to practice building a complete Angular application. I am at the beginning of my career, but I am motivated to learn, consistent, and I enjoy building things I can see and interact with."

**Tips:**
- Keep it under 90 seconds
- Mention something concrete you built
- End with why you want this internship

---

## Why do you want to do frontend development?

**Model answer:**
> "I like that the results are visible immediately. When I change the code, I can see the result in the browser right away. I also enjoy the combination of logic and design — building something that both works and looks good."

---

## Why Angular?

**Model answer:**
> "Angular is structured and opinionated, which is helpful when learning — it gives you a clear pattern to follow. It also uses TypeScript, which helps catch mistakes early. I learned that Angular is widely used in enterprise projects, so it is a practical skill to develop."

## What do you do when you get stuck?

**Model answer:**
> "First I try to understand the error message. Then I check the documentation. If I'm still stuck, I search for similar problems online. If I still can't solve it, I write down exactly what I've tried and ask for help — with as much detail as possible so the person helping me doesn't have to guess."

---

## How do you learn new technology?

**Model answer:**
> "I like to start with a short overview to understand the big picture. Then I follow a structured plan — reading theory, doing small exercises, and building something. I learn best by doing, not just reading."

---

## What are your weaknesses?

**Model answer:**
> "I am still a beginner, so there are many areas where I need more practice — especially advanced topics like state management and RxJS. But I am improving every week, and I am not afraid to ask questions or admit when I don't know something."

---

## What are your strengths?

**Model answer:**
> "I am consistent — I study every day. I ask questions when I don't understand something. And I am not easily discouraged when something is hard."

---

## Where do you see yourself in 2 years?

**Model answer:**
> "I would like to grow into a junior frontend developer who can contribute to real projects independently. I want to deepen my Angular skills, learn more about testing and performance, and become a reliable team member."

---

## Do you have any questions for us?

Always ask something. Good beginner questions:

- "What does the onboarding process look like for interns?"
- "What projects would I be working on?"
- "What does a typical day look like for a frontend intern here?"
- "What skills would you most like me to develop during the internship?"
