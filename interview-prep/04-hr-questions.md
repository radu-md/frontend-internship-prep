# Interview Prep — HR Questions

These questions are about you as a person and as a potential team member.

Practice answering out loud. Aim for 30–60 seconds per answer.

---

## Tell me about a time you worked in a team

**What they want:** Evidence you can collaborate and communicate.

**Model answer:**
> "In school/a project, I worked with others on [example]. My role was [role]. When we had a disagreement about [something], we discussed it and found a solution together. I learned that communication is more important than being right."

*Adapt this to your real experience. It does not need to be a big project.*

---

## How do you handle feedback or criticism?

**Model answer:**
> "I try to listen carefully and not react defensively. Feedback helps me improve, so I treat it as information, not as an attack. If I disagree, I ask questions to understand the reasoning before responding."

---

## Describe a time you failed or made a mistake

**Model answer:**
> "Once I spent a lot of time on a solution that turned out to be the wrong approach. I had not asked for clarification early enough. After that, I learned to confirm the requirements before building. The mistake was useful because it changed how I work."

*Be honest. Interviewers expect mistakes — they want to see how you handle them.*

---

## How do you manage your time when you have multiple tasks?

**Model answer:**
> "I write down everything I need to do and prioritize by importance and deadline. I try to complete one task fully before starting the next. When I'm unsure what to prioritize, I ask."

---

## Are you comfortable asking for help?

**Model answer:**
> "Yes — I think asking for help at the right time is better than spending hours going in the wrong direction. I always try to understand the problem and attempt a solution first, but I don't hesitate to ask if I'm stuck."

---

## What motivates you?

**Model answer:**
> "I am motivated by progress. When I learn something new and can use it to build something real, that is very satisfying. I also like having clear goals — it helps me stay focused."

---

## How do you handle pressure or tight deadlines?

**Model answer:**
> "I try to stay calm and focus on what I can control. I break the task into smaller pieces and work through them. If a deadline is at risk, I communicate early so we can adjust."

---

## Why should we choose you?

**Model answer:**
> "I am honest about what I know and what I don't. I work consistently, I ask questions when I need to, and I am motivated to learn every day. I won't pretend to know things I don't, but I will find out. I am the kind of person who improves steadily."

---

## Are you available to work full time / part time?

Be ready to answer honestly about your availability, schedule, and any constraints.

---

## Tips for HR interviews

- Speak clearly and at a normal pace — no need to rush
- It is OK to pause for a second before answering
- Short and honest is better than long and vague
- If you don't understand a question, ask them to clarify
- At the end, always ask them a question
